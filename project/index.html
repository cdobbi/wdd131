Welcome!

For years I stood firm, telling my daughter we weren't getting a rabbit, but life has a way of changing plans.

Our cat caught a tiny kit. We brought it inside, to make it comfortable in its final moments. To our surprise, the little one didn't just survive‚Äîit thrived. That marked the beginning of our journey into the surprising world of rabbitry.

People around the world show these adorable creatures competitively! üôÄ There are rabbit shows, hurdle jumping, obstacle course competitions, clubs, organizations, 4-H and other groups dedicated to rabbits. The rabbit world is delightfully enormous!

Random people, knowing we have bunnies, still bring us unwanted or feral rabbits. We quickly found ourselves with a small colony, each in need of a home.

Although we've grown our little rabbit village, breed and show Rhinelanders, Mini-Lops and Harlequins, we're still small. This allows us to focus on breeding quality pet and show animals. Our goals are to provide the best life for our rabbits and share with others, the healing that comes from owning one.
